About

So you want to learn how to fly? I can teach you. I am an aerialist that currently resides in Raleigh, NC. For those that are unfamiliar with the term aerialist, an aerialist is a person who performs aerial acrobatics sometimes with various apparatuses, such as long strands of fabric, trapeze, tightrope, metal hoops, etc.

I discovered aerial dance five years ago in Athens, GA, and later continued training under various artists in Wilmington, NC and Georgia. I have performed at dance socials for Wilmington Latin Dance, on the USS North Carolina Battleship for 2015, and now at the Rickhouse for the Mambo Dinamicoâ€™s 5th annual showcase. I love to dance, perform, and share knowledge and talents with other people.

    FAQ

      When should I pay?
    • - Payments are made before class.
      How can I pay?
    • - You can pay with cash or card.
      How can you get in contact with me for lessons or bookings?
    • - You can contact me through my social media or you can email me at comeflywithjess@gmail.com.
      Where is it?
    • - Carmen's Cuban Cafe 108 Factory Shops Rd Morrisville, NC.
      How do I sign up/set up a class?
    • - On the pricing page, there is a registration link, or you can shoot me an email and we will set up an appointment. However, everyone must sign a waiver before flying with me.
      What should I wear?
    • - Please wear comfortable athletic clothing that is preferably tight with full coverage, such as leggings, spandex, under armour, etc.
      When should I arrive?
    • - Please arrive on time or a few minutes early. Important parts of the lesson are stretching and warming up. Pulling a muscle would not let you fly with me.
      What should I expect during the lesson?
    • - You should expect at least 15 minutes of stretching and warming up before any flying. After that, I will teach you how to climb, dance, split, pose, and tie knots in the air! The last 10 minutes are for a cool down.
      Do I need prior experience for this class?
    • - Nope! I teach beginners to intermediates. You don't have to know how to do a pull-up or climb a rope, and everyone starts from the ground to prevent injury.
